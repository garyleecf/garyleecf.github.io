Angular energy distribution at the receiver. At sub-6 GHz (left), rich scattering produces energy spread across many directions — the Gaussian approximation holds reasonably well. At millimetre-wave (right), aggressive attenuation leaves only a handful of dominant paths. The channel is sparse. A model that doesn't know this pays for it.
